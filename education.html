Education

I obtained my Bachelor's degree in Civil Engineering from University of Guadalajara, Mexico, in which I specialized in the field of Structural Engineering. During my Bachelor program I participated in an International Academic Exchange for 12 months at the National Institute of Applied Sciences of Lyon, France, thanks to the prestigious MEXFITEC scholarship funding program.

I obtained the double title of Advanced Master's Degree in the Structural Analysis of Monuments and Historical Constructions (SAHC), by the Czech Technical University of Prague, Czech Republic, and by the Polytechnic University of Catalonia, Spain, thanks to the funding granted by the European Commission through the ERASMUS+ program.

At Trinity College Dublin, Ireland, I got a Postgraduate Diploma in Statistics and also a PhD in Civil, Structural and Environmental Engineering. These studies were funded by an Internal University funding Studentship Award. My doctoral research focused on the conservation of earthen vernacular architecture in Ireland through the study of the mechanical properties and the structural behavior of Cob. I carried out non-linear finite elements (FE) simulations and, in an International Research Collaboration with the University of Cantabria, Spain, an experimental campaign on the adaptation and application of the flat jack test on Cob walls.
