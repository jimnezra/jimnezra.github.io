Teaching

I participated in my first teaching experience during my my senior years of Bachelor studies when I was invited by the Student Union to facilitate a short course on computer aided design (CAD) to first year students of the Department of Civil Engineering. I found this experience to be very fulfilling and satisfactory.

As a Phd student at Trinity College Dublin, Ireland, I obtained my first formal teaching/pedagogical education by participating in several workshops and courses facilitated by the Academic Practice unit:

  • Reflective teaching assistantship course.
  • CAPSL Postgraduate teaching assistant workshop: How students learn?
  • CAPSL Postgraduate teaching assistant workshop: Session planning.

Furthermore, I received formal training on Programme Focused Assessment (from where I obtained a digital badge) and I worked as Assessment Assistant supporting Trinity Assessment Fellows to map and analyze assessment practices within Schools across the University.

I have also been involved in the design of new study programs. In 2021, I was invited by Dr. Miguel Zamora Palacios, Head of the Department of Civil and Topographic Engineering at University of Guadalajara, Mexico, to participate in the Committee of the creation of a new Master Program on Civil Engineering, whose first edition will take place on 2023.

During my PhD at Trinity College Dublin, Ireland, I worked as Teaching Assistant at the Departments of Civil, Structural and Environmental Engineering and Department of Mechanical Engineering, where I taught in the following undergraduate and graduate engineering courses:

  • 2E9 Engineering design.
  • 3A2 Structural design.
  • 2E8 Materials.
  • 3E4 Innovation and entrepreneurship.
  • 2E4 Solids and structures.
  • 4E6 Advanced theory of structures.
  • 2E4 Solids and structures.
  • 3B3 Mechanics of solids.
  • C05 Spatial analysis using GIS.

During my PhD at Trinity College Dublin, Ireland, I Organized and facilitated a series of one-day workshops on conservation, vernacular architecture, earthen construction techniques and sustainability:

  • Department of Civil, Structural and Environmental Engineering Undergraduate Students Workshop.
  • Trinity Green Week 2019 Workshop.
  • University College Dublin (UCD) and Dublin Technical University (DTU) Architecture Students Workshops.
