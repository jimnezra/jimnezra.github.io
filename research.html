Research

I am a Structural Engineer interested in the conservation of existing built heritage through inspection, diagnosis, intervention design and monitoring of buildings and bridges with historical/cultural value; the study of vernacular and sustainable architecture; exploring the mechanical properties and structural behavior of masonry, earthen and bamboo structures through the application of non-destructive testing and advanced numerical simulations; as well as in the development and implementation of new technologies in the architecture, construction and engineering (AEC) industry such as artificial intelligence, internet of things, structural health monitoring and digital twins.

I worked for two years as a Postdoctoral Researcher at Sapienza University of Rome, Italy. The research project in which I participated focused on the study of the structural behavior of masonry with the use of a non-standard limit analysis method implemented through the ALMA 2.0 program and on the development/extension of this software as well as in the study of masonry structural response by the means of a tilting table.

Currently I work as a MSCA-IF Postdoctoral Researcher at the Structural Engineering Research Group (SERG), Department of Civil Engineering and Energy Technology (CEET), Faculty of Technology, Art and Design (TDK) at the Metropolitan University of Oslo (OsloMet), in Oslo, Norway. I am the Experienced Researcher of the DTADD project.
