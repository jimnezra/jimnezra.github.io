Research

I am a Structural Engineer interested in the conservation of existing built heritage through inspection, diagnosis, intervention design and monitoring of buildings and bridges with historical/cultural value; the study of vernacular and sustainable architecture; exploring the mechanical properties and structural behavior of masonry, earthen and bamboo structures through the application of non-destructive testing, specialized constitutive models and advanced non-linear numerical simulations; robustness and resilience of the built environment; as well as in the development and implementation of new technologies in the architecture, construction and engineering (AEC) industry such as artificial intelligence, internet of things, structural health monitoring, bridge/building information modelling and digital twins. With my research, I aim at contributing to the achievement of Sustainable Development Goals 9 and 11 as adopted by the United Nations.

After obtaining internal University funding, I worked for two years as a Postdoctoral Researcher at Sapienza University of Rome, Italy. The research project in which I participated focused on the study of the structural behavior of masonry with the use of a non-standard limit analysis method implemented through the ALMA 2.0 program and on the development/extension of this software as well as in the study of masonry structural response by the means of a tilting table.

Currently I work as a MSCA-IF Postdoctoral Researcher at the Structural Engineering Research Group (SERG), Department of Civil Engineering and Energy Technology (CEET), Faculty of Technology, Art and Design (TDK) at the Metropolitan University of Oslo (OsloMet), in Oslo, Norway. I am the Experienced Researcher of the DTADD project, which has been funded by the European Union's Horizon 2020 research and innovation programme under the Marie Sklodowska-Curie grant agreement No 101066739.
